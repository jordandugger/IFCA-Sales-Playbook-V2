Episode 01
Foundations · Pre-Drill Onboarding
The IFCA Sales System Overview
~20 min
Teaching Dialogue + 2 Live Demos

Before you touch a script, you have to internalize the philosophy. This episode covers the Enrollment Advisor Creed, who IFCA is and why our system works, the three syntax types and how to pick the right one before a call, the 7 Beliefs every prospect must hold, the pre-call ritual, and why dialogue beats monologue at pitch. The new-hire's first listen.

What you'll learn
  • The Enrollment Advisor Creed: "Our job is not to sell — our job is to serve" — and why that frame is what makes you trustworthy enough to win the deal
  • The three syntax types (Goal / Problem / PT Pivot) and how to pre-map the right one from the application before you dial
  • The 7 Beliefs framework and how every objection maps back to a belief that wasn't built in discovery
  • Why the pitch is a dialogue, not a monologue — and how the Hook → Contrast → Tie-Down pattern unlocks every pillar

Episode 02
Drill · Goal Syntax (Under $2K/mo Prospect)
Marcus — The Beginner's Call
~35–45 min
Live Role-Play · 14 Beats + Debrief

Marcus is 28, works at Equinox, makes $3,750/mo and has been "trying to go online" for 8 months with no traction. Walk through the full Goal Syntax call live — discovery built around dream and gap (not problem-solving), the Cost of Inaction reveal, the Hamburger Transition, the full pitch in dialogue mode, and the Triple Tie-Down close on his hidden-fear-wearing-money-mask objection.

What you'll learn
  • How to run Goal Syntax discovery when there's no business to diagnose — desire-led, gap-exposed, fear-surfaced
  • The "Already Lost" Cost of Inaction reveal — uniquely powerful for Goal Syntax (the only calculator that quantifies past, not future, cost)
  • How to deliver Pillar 1 (Offer) in Full Mode for beginners — and why it's the primary constraint for Goal Syntax prospects
  • Triple Tie-Down on a fear-wearing-money objection — and why Tie Down #2 is the killshot that prevents enrolling someone who isn't 10/10 on process

Episode 03
Drill · PT Pivot (In-Person Trainer → Hybrid)
Kevin — The In-Person Ceiling
~35–45 min
Live Role-Play · 14 Beats + Debrief

Kevin is 32, Phoenix, an in-person PT for 8 years doing $6,400/mo with his wife on bedrest and a kid coming in 8 weeks. Walk through the three-currency PT Pivot system live — the Block 5 Cost of Inaction seed question, the no-show capture, the Knife Turn with amplification beat, the View 1 calculator reveal with all six cards including the new COI 6mo + 12mo, the Affinity Launch math reveal, and the Triple Tie-Down on his cash-flow-during-transition objection.

What you'll learn
  • How to seed the Cost of Inaction belief BEFORE the calculator drops — so View 1 becomes confirmation, not surprise
  • The three-dimensional Knife Turn for PTs: financial ceiling + physical toll + family cost — and the amplification beat that makes it stick
  • The View 1 PT Pivot reveal — six cards including no-show loss and Cost of Inaction over 6 and 12 months, sequenced for maximum impact
  • The Affinity Launch math beat that kills the "I have no audience" objection without ever having to handle it
  • Triple Tie-Down on a cash-flow-during-transition objection — the most common PT Pivot price-drop scenario

Episode 04
Drill · Problem Syntax ($2K–$40K+/mo Coach)
Danielle — The Plateaued Coach
~40–50 min
Live Role-Play · 17 Beats + Debrief

Danielle is 36, Seattle, an established online coach doing $8,200/mo plateaued for 6 months. Her CPL is $35 (3.5x the benchmark), her show rate is broken at 58%, her retention is broken at 5 months — and she's been burned by two previous programs. Walk through the diagnostic Problem Syntax call live — closer-side KPI capture, Silver Bullet diagnosis, View 2/3/4 calculator shares, and how to handle a spouse-skepticism objection with specific diagnosis (not generic guarantees).

What you'll learn
  • How to capture Problem Syntax KPIs conversationally — without making it feel like an audit
  • The closer-side View 1 diagnostic (DO NOT share) and how to read severity tiers to find the Silver Bullet
  • The View 2 / View 3 / View 4 share sequence — showing only the constraint, then the leak math, then the Silver Bullet ROI reveal
  • Why "the other programs didn't diagnose the constraint" is the most underused handle for spouse-skepticism after past failed investments
  • How Problem Syntax pitch differs from Goal — Pillar 5 (Retention) lands harder for established coaches because they feel retention loss viscerally

Episode 05
Master Class · Objection Handling
The Triple Tie-Down & Every Major Handle
~25–30 min
Framework Teaching + Live Role-Play Examples

The objection-handling master class. Covers the Master Sequence isolation rule, the full Triple Tie-Down on money (the killshot framework, drilled live), the "I need to think about it" fear-objection sequence, the spouse handle, the ego objection, complaints vs. objections, and the closer mindset rules that override every specific handle. Re-listen to this episode between every drill — the Triple Tie-Down decays without repetition.

What you'll learn
  • The Master Sequence — the two-question isolation that pivots you from fighting the prospect to problem-solving with them (used FIRST on every objection)
  • The full Triple Tie-Down on money — Tie Down #1 (isolate), Tie Down #2 (the hypothetical killshot), Permission Bridge, Open Wallet (all three buckets), Tie Down #3 (custom plan close with "treat you like you paid in full")
  • The "I need to think about it" four-step sequence — clarify, isolate, reframe, cost flip — and why fear-based objections never become follow-ups
  • The spouse objection paths (A vs. B), the ego objection social judo, and why complaints ≠ objections
  • The closer mindset rules that override every framework — silence as weapon, simplicity over complexity, gamify don't defend
